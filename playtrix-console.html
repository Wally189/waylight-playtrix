Clients & Sales

A single place to watch the live pipeline, active client relationships, weekly sales rhythm, and the next commercial move.

iSales playbook and examples

Plain commercial flow: keep people and organisations in Contacts, keep pre-approval opportunities in the Lead pipeline, move approved work into Projects, and keep live delivery or maintenance relationships in Client relationships.

Capture contact Reach out Discuss or demo Qualify Proposal Approve to project Deliver or maintain Close or archive

What lives where

Contacts is the directory. Lead pipeline is the commercial opportunity before yes. Projects begins when the work is approved. Client relationships is for the live relationship once delivery or maintenance is real.

How to move something forward

Every lead needs a stage, a named next action, and a date in the business diary if the date matters. If there is no next action, it is not live. If the answer is no, close it plainly. If the answer is yes, approve it into Projects.

When to close something

Close the lead if the fit is wrong, the budget is not there, the reply has gone cold after reasonable follow-up, or the work is not worth pursuing. Closure is better than a false pipeline.

Data retention and public information

Even if you found a name or email on a public site, your notes about that person are still personal data if they identify them. Keep only what you need, record why you are keeping it, and review or delete stale leads after a sensible retention period.

Governance

Practical controls for a very small business

Use Governance to keep the rules, repeatable methods, review dates, compliance checks, and people record index in one working layer. It only needs to answer three questions: what is the rule, what needs review next, and is the business compliant enough for its current size?

iHow this area is split

Policies hold rules and standards. SOPs hold formal repeatable procedures. Process Notes hold lighter guidance, exceptions, and reminders. People records here are the index and review surface only; sensitive files stay in the restricted secure location.

PoliciesRules, standards, and control expectations.
SOPsFormal repeatable procedures for delivery and operations.
Process NotesPractical guidance, reminders, and known exceptions.

Finance & Treasury

Practical money control for a sole-trader business

Use Finance to see the current picture, keep the core money registers current, plan pricing and forecasts, track reserves, and stay organised for tax and reporting without turning Playtrix into accounting software.

iHow this area is read

Client revenue is money earned from clients. Owner Contribution is money introduced personally to fund the business and should stay visible, but separate from true client earnings. Forecasts, cash position, and reserves are planning controls, not formal accounts.

Client revenueMoney earned from websites, maintenance, advisory work, and other client services.
Owner ContributionPersonal funding introduced to support business activity without inflating earned revenue.
Reserves and reportingCash discipline, tax set-aside, and the admin rhythm that keeps the business orderly.
