GDPR

Data rights and legal basis

This page summarises GDPR-related rights and legal context for the Waylight Atlantic website.

For the full processing overview, including cookies and third-party services, see the Privacy Policy.

Controller and scope

Who is responsible for data processing

Data controller: Waylight Atlantic

Location: Bristol, United Kingdom

Privacy contact route: Use the Contact page. A dedicated privacy email is not currently published on this site.

Processing Summary

What is processed and the legal basis used

Processing activity Legal basis
Contact enquiry handling (Formspree) Legitimate interests and, where applicable, pre-contract steps at your request.
Technical website operation and security logs Legitimate interests in secure, reliable website operation.
Optional pricing preference storage Legitimate interests in providing a functional pricing display experience.

Not currently in use: No analytics platform, advertising pixels, newsletter tooling, embedded map, or embedded video by default.

Data Subject Rights

Your rights under UK GDPR and EU GDPR

  1. Access: Request a copy of personal data held about you.

  2. Rectification: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.

  3. Erasure: Request deletion where there is no valid reason to continue processing.

  4. Restriction and objection: Request limits on processing or object where lawful.

  5. Portability: Request transfer of data where this right applies.

To make a rights request, use the Contact page and mark your request as GDPR/privacy related.

Regulators and Law

Supervisory authorities and legislation

United Kingdom regulator: Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

Ireland regulator: Data Protection Commission (DPC).

United Kingdom legislation: UK GDPR text on legislation.gov.uk and the Data Protection Act 2018 (UK).

Ireland legislation: EU GDPR text on EUR-Lex and the Data Protection Act 2018 (Ireland).
